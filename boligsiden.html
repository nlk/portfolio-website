Boligsiden
Website redesign

Project description:

Boligsiden, a real estate search page, helps people get an overview of the housing market. Boligsiden has existed since 1999 and had not really had their homepage updated for 10 years. After launching their new app they wanted to take a look at their website content. I worked on updating the page they called the ‘house presentation page’.

Project process:

During my 2 months at boligsiden I organised user interviews and based on my findings creating a report on what the users liked/disliked and their general behavior and interaction with the site. Based on my findings I worked on organizing and getting an overview of what was already available on the site. Many items where useful, but they needed to be presented at relevant times.

Skills acquired/Used:

I was responsible for setting up user interviews, script, finding participants, organise and executing interviews, organizing the information and findings into a report afterwards. As a result of this research I worked on getting an overview of the actual content on the ‘house presentations page’ and consider how the information could be reorganized intuitively and in a relevant order. Lastly I worked on a low fidelity wireframe of the page, with suggestions of how to layout the page. These wireframes were then passed on to a graphic designer for further work and designing.

Mid fidelity wireframes:​

We wanted to experiment with different menu versions as it was discovered many users did not scroll down the presentation page. We hoped that by making the menu bar more visual that it would give the user a better overview. I also experimented with different layout versions, that provided a flexibility site layout for future updates and add ons.

Value proposition:

In order to decide on which content should or should not be shown on the new ‘house presentation site’ we worked on getting an overview of what the users needed in regards to the user interviews I had previously done. This was done by creating a value proposition over the interview findings and what the site already offered the users.

Organizing site content:​

With the user research and value proposition done we agreed that we would start updating the site, by looking at what was already on the site. Here we evaluating what items where relevant and in which relation it should be shown. With these three sheets I organized the content of the existing page into relevant categories and subcategories.

Mid-fidelity wireframe of site content and layout:

Lastly I created an example of how the site could be layed out. Here I focused on how the information on the site could be structured in a relevant, visual, and intuitive way, so that the user had the right information at the time is was presented to them, based on my previous research and findings.
